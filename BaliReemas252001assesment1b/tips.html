aList of some Living Style Guide tips below state.....

  • ➣ Start by documenting the layout of the page (Debenham 2011)
  • ➣ Record all the basics such as fonts, sizes, colours and more
  • ➣ Create one generic template and use it for mutlipul websites. Just learn to adapt it to the needed cirumstances(Haritonvo 2015)
  • ➣ Note down patterns used in the site
  • ➣ Add interactive elevement such as hovering message text to help readers better understand the guide. The hover messages can be a more indepth look on a topic, and opinion on the topic or simply an alternate to why its used or justification.(Lambert 2016)
  • ➣ Write it like it's meant for someone else, not for you (Debenham 2011)
  • ➣ Get input regarding the guide from other members of your team is always a good idea, don't forget those team members and anyone who will later join the team will be refering to it (Lambert 2016)
  • ➣ Make sure the guide is clean, easy to read and easy to update as it will change everytime anything is added or taken away from the project.(Lambert 2016)
  • ➣ Make sure people within your team working on the project are aware of the guide. If they don't know it exsists no one will use and that then it's a waste. So make sure anyone new coming into the team as well as all the others working on the project are fully aware that this tool is at their disposal.(Haritonvo 2015)
